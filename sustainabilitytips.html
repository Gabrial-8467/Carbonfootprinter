Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Always recycle when possible and try to reduce single-use items.

Reduce

Reduce your use of single-use plastics like bags and bottles by using reusable containers instead.

Reuse

Instead of throwing something away after using it once, ask yourself if there's any way you can reuse it. You can reuse a paper towel roll by putting it in your hamster's cage so he can crawl through and chew on it. You can reuse containers like glass jars and plastic tubs by storing leftovers in them. You can even reuse kitchen scraps by composting them and using the compost in your garden.

Recycle

More than 35 million tons of plastics were generated in the United States in 2018 and only 8.7 percent was recycled. Some types of plastics are not accepted in community recycling programs. Check with your local recycling program to find out which types of plastic they accept. When possible, purchase products made from recycled plastic materials.

Conserve Water

Fix leaks, take shorter showers, and only run the dishwasher and washing machine with full loads.


Fix Leaks:

Regularly check for and fix any leaks in faucets, toilets, and pipes.

Low-Flow Fixtures:

Install low-flow showerheads and faucets to reduce water usage.

Dual-Flush Toilets:

Consider replacing older toilets with dual-flush models to save water.

Turn Off Faucets:

Turn off the faucet while brushing your teeth, washing your face, or shaving.

Shorter Showers:

Aim for shorter showers, and use a timer to track your time.

Collect Rainwater:

Use rain barrels to collect rainwater for watering plants and gardens.

Use a Broom:

Instead of hosing down driveways and sidewalks, use a broom to clean them.

Only Run Full Loads:

Run your dishwasher and washing machine only with full loads.

Fix Dripping Faucets:

Repair any dripping faucets promptly.

Water Plants Efficiently:

Water plants early in the morning or late in the evening to minimize evaporation.

Mulch:

Apply mulch around plants to retain soil moisture.

Choose Drought-Tolerant Plants:

Select plants that require less water for your garden.

Adjust Sprinklers:

Adjust sprinklers to avoid watering sidewalks and streets.

Use a Pool Cover:

Cover your pool to reduce evaporation.

Compost:

Compost kitchen waste to improve soil quality and water retention.

Install a Smart Irrigation System:

Consider a smart irrigation system that adjusts watering based on weather and soil conditions.

Harvest Greywater:

Reuse greywater (from sinks and showers) for watering plants.

Educate Yourself:

Educate yourself and others about water conservation.

Fix Broken Sprinklers:

Repair broken or misaligned sprinklers to avoid wasting water.

Insulate Pipes:

Choose Energy-Efficient Appliances: Energy-efficient appliances use less water and energy.
